Math Magic

One thing that I unfortunately see lacking today is the ability to do math without using a calculator. Calculators are found everywhere, on phones, on watches... it kind of deprives a person of the convenience and speed of doing the math themselves. I'm proud to say I have not lost that ability. I learned many math tricks to help speed up any calculation.


For example, here is a trick taught by Scott Flansburg, known by his fans as the "Human Calculator":


Multiplying two numbers that are close to 100, but less than 100

Example:
  96 
x 94 
____ 

"The first thing you notice is that both numbers are pretty close to 100. Quickly figure out the difference between each number and 100. The difference between 96 and 100 is 4. The difference between 94 and 100 is 6. Let's write these numbers down like this:"
Example:
  96  4
x 94  6
____ 
"Now, the second step is to subtract diagonally. By that I mean subtract 6 from 96, or 4 from 94. 96 - 6 = 90. Write that down under the first two numbers. (Note that this is the same as 94 minus 4. It does not matter which you choose. Whichever you diagonally subtract, you get the same number.)"
Example:
  96  4
x 94  6
____ 
  90 
"In the third step, we multiply the two numbers we got by figuring the difference between our original numbers and 100: 4 and 6. 4 x 6 = 24. Write that to the right of 90."
Example:
  96  4
x 94  6
____ __
  90 24
"Got that? Guess what? You've got the answer: 9024"
Example:
  96  4
x 94  6
____ __
  9024

Multiplying two numbers that are close to 100, but more than 100

As described by Mr Flansburg, you do the same thing here, finding the difference between the numbers and 100, but instead of subtracting diagonally, you add diagonally. The rest you do the same way.

Example:
  103 
x 107 
_____ 
  103  3
x 107  7
_____ 
  103  3
x 107  7
_____ 
  110 
  103  3
x 107  7
_____ __
  110 21
  103  3
x 107  7
_____ __
  11021

Answer: 11021

Multiplying two numbers that are close to 100, one is above, the other below

Now this was not described by our friend the Human Calculator. I figured the method for this one on my own. It's a little more complicated, but you get the hang of it.

Example:
   93 
x 108 
_____ 

Do the same thing: find the difference between each number and 100.

Example:
   93  7
x 108  8
_____ 

Here's where it gets a little tricky. Do you add or subtract? Well, if it's the smaller number, you add. If it's the bigger number, you subtract. You'll end up with the same result. 108 - 7 = 101. 93 + 8 = 101.

Example:
   93  7
x 108  8
_____ 
  101 

Next step is again a little more complicated. 7 x 8 = 56, but we can't just write 101 to the left and 56 to the right of it. That gives us 10156, which is wrong. In the first example, 96 x 94, you're essentially taking 9000 (90 times 100) and adding 24 to it, i.e. 9024. But there, we need to take 101 times 100 i.e. 10100, and subtract 56 from it. That gives us the answer.

Example:
   93  7
x 108  8
_____ __
  101 00
    - 56
  100 44

Answer: 10044

If the two numbers are... Do this diagonally, then... Multiply the difference numbers and do this with the product:
both below 100 subtract put it to the right of the left number (same as multiplying it by 100 then adding)
both above 100 add put it to the right of the left number (same as multiplying it by 100 then adding)
one is above the other below subtract if the larger, add if the smaller multiply the left number by 100 and subtract the product from that
